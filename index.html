STRATEGIC INTENT

Dynamic capabilities require direction. Strategic intent — the organization's goals, competitive aspirations, and working assumptions — defines what knowledge to seek, how to interpret it, and when to act.

1
Knowledge Creation & Absorption
How external knowledge is generated, acquired, and brought into the organization
→ Resource Picking (Barney, 1991) — AI expands the scanning aperture across sources and geographies
2
Knowledge Integration
How dispersed knowledge gets combined into coherent, actionable intelligence
→ Resource Bundling (Sirmon et al., 2007) — Ghoshal's three clusters operationalize this
3
Knowledge Reconfiguration
How the organization adapts its CI practices and strategic responses over time
→ Capability Building (Teece et al., 1997) — adapting CI practices = building dynamic capabilities

These three capabilities operate throughout the CI process — not as sequential stages, but as continuous knowledge dynamics enabled by organizational resources.

COMPETITOR ANALYSIS & DYNAMICSTHEORETICAL FRAMEWORK
CORE CONTRIBUTION

Integrates competitor analysis (market commonality + resource similarity) with interfirm rivalry through AMC. First attempt to bridge the prebattle competitive relationship with prediction of rivalrous behavior.

KEY INSIGHTS
• Market commonality and resource similarity shape tension — behavior depends on awareness, motivation, capability
• Competition is multi-level: global (firm level) and focal (specific market)
• Competitive asymmetry: rivals don't pose equal threats to each other
Research gap Framework is static — how AMC drivers evolve dynamically remains unexplored.

"The joint consideration of market commonality and resource similarity shows the complementarity of two prominent but contrasting strategy theories."

APPLICATION TO THESIS

AMC drivers map to what AI-driven CI must capture: awareness, motivation, capability. Lens for survey/interview design.

COMPETITOR ANALYSIS & DYNAMICSEMPIRICAL — QUANTITATIVE
CORE CONTRIBUTION

Introduces competitor acumen: the extent to which a focal firm's assessment of a rival's prioritization of competitors reflects the rival's own view.

KEY INSIGHTS
• A firm's prioritization reflects underlying strategic thinking with resource allocation implications
• Competitor acumen shaped by relational and structural competitive embeddedness
• High acumen + high resource deployment = greatest market share gains
• "Competitor acumen is even more important in today's dynamic environment"

"Competitor acumen: the extent to which a focal firm's assessment of a given rival's prioritization of competitors reflects the rival's own view."

Research gap Single point in time, U.S. airlines only. Authors call for "longitudinal database" and "dynamic model of competitor acumen."
APPLICATION TO THESIS

Measurable construct for interviews/surveys. AI-driven CI should increase competitor acumen — a potential DV or mediating construct.

STRATEGY PROCESSSYSTEMATIC LIT REVIEW (991)

KEY INSIGHTS
• Implementation skills can be learned; contingent on organizational dynamics
• Success depends on commitment, learning, and key-person involvement
• Strategists' cognitive models associated with outcomes
• "Implementing strategy means managing change. Change may hinder effectiveness"

"Research on implementation is seen as inferior compared with formulation. This is incomprehensible because implementation is the most important challenge."

Research gap (1) "Link implementation characteristics to outcomes." (2) "Establish a link between formulation and implementation." No implementation taxonomies after 991 articles.
APPLICATION TO THESIS

Directly parallels our CIO framework (Capabilities → Implementation → Outcomes). Validates focus on implementation as the underexplored frontier.

STRATEGY PROCESSFOUNDATIONAL THEORY
CORE CONTRIBUTION

Distinguishes deliberate strategy (intended and realized) from emergent strategy (patterns without intention). Eight types along the continuum.

KEY INSIGHTS
• Emergent strategy = "strategic learning" — responding to evolving reality
• Pattern recognition is crucial to effective managers
• "Umbrella strategy" maps to AI-driven CI — leaders set boundaries, details emerge
Research gap Strategies more deliberate in centrally controlled orgs; more emergent in decentralized — does AI centralize or decentralize this?

"Our conclusion is that strategy formation walks on two feet, one deliberate, the other emergent."

APPLICATION TO THESIS

AI excels at pattern recognition — does it make emergent strategies more visible and deliberately manageable?
